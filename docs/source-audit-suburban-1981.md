# Chevrolet Suburban 1981 exterior-color source audit

## Decision

The official 1981 GM Heritage Vehicle Information Kit contains a complete
Suburban “Interior and Exterior Color Availability Chart” at PDF page 26,
printed Suburban Page H. The chart is dated August 8, 1980. The public
solid-color timeline publishes the 15 boldface primary exterior colors and does
not treat lightface secondary rows as standalone offerings.

The Silverado-only marks attach to interior trim codes XRR1, LRR1, and LTT1.
They do not restrict an exterior color.

## Separate two-tone evidence

PDF page 27 documents eight optional Special Two-Tone ZY3 and Exterior Decor
Package ZY5 combinations. ZY3 uses primary color above the moldings and on the
roof, with secondary color below. ZY5 reverses the primary and secondary body
placement and adds coordinated striping.

| Primary code and color | Secondary code and color | Stripe colors | Interior color |
|---|---|---|---|
| 17 Light Silver Metallic | 86 Midnight Black | Vermillion/Black | Slate |
| 18 Charcoal Metallic | 17 Light Silver Metallic | Vermillion/Light Slate | Slate |
| 25 Light Blue Metallic | 17 Light Silver Metallic | Dark Blue/Light Slate | Blue or Slate |
| 30 Nordic Blue Metallic | 17 Light Silver Metallic | Vermillion/Light Slate | Blue or Slate |
| 65 Dark Chestnut Metallic | 60 Santa Fe Tan | Doeskin/Santa Fe Tan | Doeskin |
| 70 Carmine Red | 71 Dark Carmine Red | Vermillion/Dark Carmine Red | Carmine or Slate |
| 70 Carmine Red | 71 Dark Carmine Red | Doeskin/Dark Carmine Red | Doeskin |
| 86 Midnight Black | 17 Light Silver Metallic | Vermillion/Light Slate | Slate |

The two Carmine Red/Dark Carmine Red rows are separate source combinations with
different stripe and interior packages. These records remain a distinct
paint-scheme evidence class and do not create standalone color availability.
