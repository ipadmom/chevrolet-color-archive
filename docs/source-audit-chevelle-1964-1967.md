# 1964–1967 Chevelle exterior-color source audit

This audit reviews the complete exterior-color and interior-trim charts in the
four dedicated GM Heritage Chevelle kits. The public matrix contains 59
year-specific solid-color listings normalized into 48 exact-name timeline rows.
It does not import colors from a general Chevrolet chart.

Two-tone combinations are preserved below as a separate evidence class. They
are not additional solid colors and are not promoted into the current matrix.

## Controlling charts

| Year | Locator | Revision | Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1964 | PDF pp. 26–27, printed `BODY-3` and `4-BODY` | October 1963 | 14 solid rows listed; Goldwood Yellow withheld |
| 1965 | PDF pp. 35–36, printed Section II, pp. 21–22 | March 1, 1965 | 12 listed; 3 Malibu S.S.-only rows restricted |
| 1966 | PDF pp. 40–41, printed `BODY-3–4` | Revised December 1965 | 15 solid rows listed |
| 1967 | PDF pp. 48–52, printed `BODY-3–7` | September 1966 | 15 solid rows listed |

The March 1, 1965 instructions control over the earlier September chart because
they add three colors for Malibu S.S. while continuing to exclude those colors
from Malibu, 300 Deluxe, and 300. The 1964 numeric values are RPO ordering
codes. From 1965 forward, repeated letters identify solid exterior colors.

## Timeline normalization

The solid inventory has 59 year listings. Exact-name normalization produces 48
rows:

- Tuxedo Black and Ermine White each span 1964–1966.
- Mist Blue, Danube Blue, Willow Green, Artesian Turquoise, Madeira Maroon,
  Regal Red, and Cameo Beige each span 1965–1966.
- All other names remain year-specific.

Marketing names are not merged from color family, code reuse, or an
interpretive screen swatch. A color enters the public matrix only after its
complete chart has been reviewed.
